Terrible Poetry

Description

Tweedledum and Tweedledee!

Who are you, my little dear?

Alice?… What a name!

No, you cannot pass,

what a shame!

Oh no, don’t be mad…

We did not mean to make you sad.

Listen now, here is a deal.

The blockade law,

we can repeal.

We have a box,

filled with cards,

the deck protected,

by logic guards…

At least last time we checked,

’cause last night, we got a bit wrecked.

And so we mingled and mangled,

we jingled and jangled,

all our precious cards.

On the back of them are numbers,

On the front are just hearts.

Some numbers are odd,

but others are not.

Some hearts of pure gold,

others rusty and old.

But what makes them common,

what is the law?

When gold can be found,

nothing even’s around.

Now it’s your round,

your only way out:

make sure the rule’s sound,

flipping just few cards around.

Input/Output Description

Input

A list xs of two-sided cards (coloured hearts and numbers) where you can only see one side.

Output

The number of cards you must flip in order to check if Tweedledum and Tweedledee’s law is valid: Every card with a golden heart has no even number on its opposite side.

Constraints

  • \(0 \le |xs| \le 10^6\)
