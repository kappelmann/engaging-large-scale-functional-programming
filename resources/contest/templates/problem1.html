Our innovative students from the TUM School of Money have come up with a revolutionary startup idea. In order to win the game of capitalism, they now need to maximise the revenue by selling their product to the world.

So far, there are \(n\) interested customers. The \(i\)-th customer is willing to pay at most \(a_i\) euros for the product. If the price for the product is \(m\) euros, then the total money the startup earns is \(m\) multiplied by the number of people who are willing to pay \(m\) euros or more.

Help the capitalistic TUM spin-off maximising their revenue.

Input/Output Description

Input

The input parameter is a list of \(n\) integers, the \(i\)-th integer representing the price that the \(i\)-th customer is willing to pay.

Output

Your function should return one integer - the maximum income the startup can achieve.

Constraints

  • \(1 \le n \le 10000\)
  • \(1 \le a_i \le 1012\)

Examples

Input

  1. [1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8]
  2. [2,4,2]

Output

  1. 20
  2. 6
